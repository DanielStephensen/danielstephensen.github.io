Planar Convex Hulls

Bachelor project in Computer Science investigating algorithms for computing planar convex hulls. The thesis provides a detailed analysis of algorithmic approaches to the convex hull problem, including correctness proofs and both upper and lower asymptotic complexity bounds.

  • Investigated the landscape of planar convex hull algorithms, from the classical gift-wrapping method to optimal output-sensitive algorithms.
  • Proved correctness and analysed the time complexity of each algorithm discussed.
  • Proved the lower bound time complexity for the planar convex hull problem of Ω(n log n).
  • Proved that there exist a randomized algorithm with an expected time complexity of O(n) for a large range of inputs.
  • Implemented selected algorithms and verified their performance on a range of input distributions.
