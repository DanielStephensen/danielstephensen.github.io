Planar Convex Hulls

Bachelor project in Computer Science investigating algorithms for computing planar convex hulls. The thesis provides a rigorous analysis of classical and modern algorithmic approaches, including their correctness proofs and asymptotic complexity bounds.

  • Surveyed the landscape of planar convex hull algorithms, from the classical gift-wrapping method to optimal output-sensitive algorithms.
  • Proved correctness and analysed the time complexity of each algorithm discussed.
  • Implemented selected algorithms and verified their behaviour on a range of input distributions.
  • Presented and defended the work to a faculty examination committee.
